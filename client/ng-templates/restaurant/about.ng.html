About Ristorante Bella Vita

Ristorante Bella Vita is an authentic Italian style diner located on the charming 1'st Street of the historic downtown of Los Altos. Established in the early 90's and rebuilt in 2003, Bella Vita has been a host and a popular destinations in the Bay Area for those who come to shop, stroll, wine, dine and experience way-of-life in Los Altos. While chef Seamus works committedly with the local organic farms to deliver the best and the freshest seasonal ingredients; the ever so popular Jan (pronounced John), manages the floor to ensure complete client satisfaction and experience.

Jan - manager

Jan, a Los Altos resident, spent the better part of his youth traveling the world and experiencing different cultures and their food. From the street gritty to the most prestigious, he worked under a wide variety of restaurateurs in the hopes that some day he would open his very own restaurant. Since then, he has built 15 successful restaurants and looks forward to building more in the future. Bella Vita has a special place in Jan's heart because he loves being in Los Altos and enjoys the long lasting relationships he built with the local clientele serving them since the late 90's.

Shamus - chef

Presentation and pallet are two peas in a pod in Shamus's kitchen. "The absence of either is only half the experience, and experience does not exist in halves," he states eloquently in his broken English. For Shamus, eating is an experience even when it is a grilled-cheese sandwich. Of course, when it is prepared by Shamus, even a simple grilled-cheese sandwich is anything but a simple experience and an explosive delight. Starting his career in a butchers shop before adolescence, at an early age Shamus developed a fascination and a discerning eye for quality in food. Over the years, cooking became a natural extension of this passion, igniting his career as a renowned chef in the Bay Area.

Miguel - chef

“The connection between a creative thought in my mind and the ability to develop the concept into a finished product using my hands has always attracted me,” says Miguel. “Cooking became a natural extension of this passion, igniting my career.” Growing up in a food-focused household, where formal, white tablecloth dinners were a bi-weekly event, Miguel learned early on that a meal was an experience with a soul, bringing people together to share their lives. He began his career at the age of 16 working his way through several restaurants, developing skills for a career that he claims, “chose me as much as I chose it.”
